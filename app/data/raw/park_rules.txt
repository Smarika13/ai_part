Safety rules for visitors inside the park.
Visitors must always stay with their authorized guide and must never wander off alone inside the park.
A safe distance must be maintained from all wildlife, especially elephants, rhinoceros, and tigers.
Visitors should avoid making loud noises or sudden movements near animals.
All instructions given by guides must be followed immediately during wildlife encounters.
Visitors must remain inside designated safari vehicles and should not exit without permission from the guide.

Rules for wildlife protection.
Feeding animals inside the park is strictly prohibited.
Littering is not allowed, and visitors must carry all trash back with them.
Picking flowers, plants, or collecting any natural materials from the park is prohibited.
Photography is allowed for personal use, but flash photography near animals is not permitted.
Visitors must not disturb animal habitats, nests, or breeding areas.

Park entry rules and restrictions.
All visitors must carry valid entry permits at all times inside the park.
Park entry is only allowed during designated hours, from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
Children under the age of 10 must be accompanied by an adult at all times.
Smoking and consumption of alcohol are strictly prohibited inside the park.
Drones and unauthorized filming or recording equipment are not allowed without special permission.

Conservation and responsible tourism guidelines.
Visitors should respect local communities and their traditional rights.
Supporting sustainable and eco-friendly tourism practices is strongly encouraged.
Any suspicious activities or signs of poaching should be reported immediately to park authorities.
Visitors must stay on marked trails and designated areas only.
Bringing plastic bags or non-biodegradable materials into the park is discouraged.

Emergency procedures inside the park.
In case of an emergency, visitors should contact park rangers or their guide immediately.
The official emergency contact number for Chitwan National Park is +977-56-580291.
First aid stations are available at the park headquarters and major entry points.
Visitors should always inform someone about their itinerary before entering the park.
