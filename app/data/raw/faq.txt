
Chitwan National Park FAQs

Q: When was Chitwan National Park established?
A: Chitwan National Park was established in 1973 as Nepal's first national park.

Q: What animals can I see in Chitwan?
A: You can see one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers, elephants, crocodiles, leopards, sloth bears, and over 500 species of birds.

Q: What is the best time to visit?
A: The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and wildlife viewing is optimal.

Q: What activities are available?
A: Jungle safaris, elephant rides, canoe trips, bird watching, and cultural programs are available.

Q: How much is the entry fee?
A: For foreigners, the entry fee is approximately NPR 1500 per person per day.
